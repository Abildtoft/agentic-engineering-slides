# Digital Empowerment of Primary Care

The healthcare system faces significant challenges due to an aging population and a rising prevalence of chronic diseases.

‍

As a result, the demand for primary care providers (PCPs) is growing.

However, limited access to specialists and an overwhelming administrative workload have contributed to burnout and dissatisfaction among PCPs.

Our vision is to create a digital PCP companion that provides direct access to specialist advice and enhanced administrative support.

This tool will improve the effectiveness and satisfaction of professionals working in primary care medicine.

Team

Our dedicated team brings together expertise from medical education, software development, engineering, business development, law and design. We collaborate closely with leading clinicians, hospitals, researchers from Danmarks Tekniske Universitet (DTU) and the Niels Bohr Institute as well as the Danish Regions.

Research involvement

Melatech is partner of multiple research projects aimed at advancing healthcare technology. As a partner in the Artificial Intelligence in Smart Clinical research consortium (AISC), we focus on integrating AI-driven solutions to enhance clinical decision-making.

Through the Dermoscopic Augmented Histology Trial (DAHT), we investigate whether providing pathologists with pre-removal images of lesions improves the accuracy and efficiency of histopathological evaluations.

Our involvement in SynTIA contributes to developing an AI system to assist dermatologists in diagnosing skin cancer more quickly and accurately.

Additionally, our collaboration on PRSONAL is dedicated to personalizing healthcare delivery through AI-driven insights, optimizing patient care and resource allocation.

### Martin G. Tolsgaard

MD, PhD, Dr Med Sci and Associate Professor of Medical Education

Bringing together large-scale image data with the science of learning is a crucial next step for medical education in the next decades to come. The way we have trained and taught students and health care professional until now has been based on a 'one size fits all' approach, and not based on the learning needs of individuals. ### Lisbet Rosenkrantz Hølmich

MD, DMSc, Professor of Plastic Surgery, Chair of the Danish Melanoma Group

Dermloop has the potential of revolutionizing skin cancer management. The AI augmented individual learning will bypass the ordinary training in diagnosing skin cancer and especially melanocytic lesions, which notoriously is very demanding. The prospect of getting rapid feedback on all photographed lesions in the clinic, either handled in own practice or referred, will ensure rapid learning, pattern recognition, and secure much better patient treatment. With the huge increase in skin cancer, both melanoma and non-melanoma, in Denmark as well as in the entire western world, we need all the help we can get to manage this “tsunami” of patient referrals.
